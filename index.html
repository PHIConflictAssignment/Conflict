Resolving Conflict

What is Conflict?

Conflict is a common occurrence in everyday life and can best be described as discordant ideas or feelings that are expressed or experienced between two interdependent parties. (Communicating in Groups: Applications and Skills 272)

Whether the conflict arises at home or in the office, steps can be taken to ensure that your next conflict increases cohesiveness, adds to member involvement, improves the groups decisions and increases awareness on how to help other members understand issues more clearly.

How can conflict be resolved?

There are five conflict management styles that help to improve a group’s ability to collectively achieve its goal. These are Avoidance, Accommodation, Competition, Compromise, and Collaboration.

The most common conflict situations at work fall into the following three categories:
Substantive Conflict

Substantive conflict, which revolves around the task at hand, where members conceptualize, analyze, and evaluate ideas and information. This type of conflict can be beneficial to team growth.

Affective Conflict

Affective conflict stems from interpersonal power clashes. They can be work related or personal opinions and views. This type of conflict can detour a team from its timeline and goals.

Procedural Conflict

Procedural conflict arises from the “how” the group should do things, including how the group will make future decisions and meeting times.

Conclusion

In conclusion, conflict occurs everywhere you go in everything you do. It is impossible to avoid and can be best utilized when effective proactive steps are taken. We all know how challenging it can be to step up and make waves. Thus, it is imperative in a group situation that everyone finds a happy medium, which allows for a flexible give and take relationship, where each members ideas and views can be analyzed and evaluated to come up with the best option for the team.
